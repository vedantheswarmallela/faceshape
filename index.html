Decoding Styling Products

The difference between pomade, clay, and wax can make or break a style. Here is your quick reference:

Product Type Hold Level Shine Finish Best Suited For Key Benefit
Pomade (Water-based) Medium to Strong High / Wet Shine Slick backs, pompadours, classic parts Easy to wash out, sleek formal looks
Hair Clay High / Firm Matte / Natural Textured crops, quiffs, messy/natural looks Adds volume, absorbs oil, natural movement
Hair Wax Medium to High Low to Medium Spiky hair, faux hawks, active days Pliable hold (can restyle throughout day)
Styling Powder Light to Medium Matte / Invisible Fine hair, crop fades, quick textured look Instant root volume and dry texture
Sea Salt Spray Light / Flexible Matte / Natural Curtain parts, wavy locks, pre-styling base Enhances natural waves, adds beachy texture

How to Talk to Your Barber

  • Bring photos: A visual reference is worth a thousand words. Show photos from our Catalog.
  • Describe your routine: Tell them if you spend 2 minutes or 20 minutes styling in the morning.
  • Specify sides and top: Use terms like "taper fade" or "number 2 on the sides" if you know your preferences.

Heat & Blow Dryer Secrets

  • Use a blow dryer: Heat sets the root direction. Crucial for quiffs and pompadours!
  • Finish with cool air: Use the cool shot button on your dryer to lock the style in place.
  • Pre-stylers: Apply a light spray or cream on damp hair before blow drying for extra protection and structure.

Hair Washing & Health

  • Don't daily shampoo: Strips natural oils. Wash 2-3 times a week, and condition daily.
  • Match your scalp type: Use tea tree shampoos for oily hair, and moisturizing shampoos for dry hair.
  • Towel dry gently: Rubbing aggressively breaks hair fibers. Pat dry with a towel instead.
